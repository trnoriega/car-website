Mitochondrial Tubules and Nucleoids

Christof Osman, a post-doctoral researcher in my lab, developed a way to make high resolution 3-dimensional (3D) microscope images of single bakerâ€™s yeast cells. The mitochondrial tubule network (red) and the mitochondrial DNA nucleoids (green) can be clearly seen:

Our challenge was to take these images and develop a set of computational tools that would allow analysis of hundreds of the images to determine whether the green spots were randomly distributed along the red network.

The first step was to convert the microscope images into 3D coordinates that could be interpreted by Matlab scripts:

We could then analyze the 3D cell coordinates to quantitate the properties of each cell. For example, we could determine the distances between all the mitochondrial nucleoids, the distance between each nucleoid and the nearest mitochondrial tip, or the length of each mitochondrial network. These quantitative analyses then allowed us to test how perturbing different cellular functions affected mitochondrial shape and nucleoid number.

This work led to the publication of a research article (download here) in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences on February 2015.
