Dynamic ribosomes and dynamic signal recognition particles

I developed a system to visualize thousands of individual static ribosomes (red spots) interacting with thousands of individual signal recognition particles (SRPs, green spots). Each experiment generates 5-10 movies like this:

(The inset with a blue border is a zoom-in of the spots surrounded by the yellow rectangle.

I converted each of these movies into data sets to analyze and understand how SRPs interact with ribosomes over time. Using scripts (like this one) I cleaned up the data sets to only include and analyze spots that conveyed useful information. For example, the image below shows a single ribosome (represented by the red line) interacting with two SRP molecules (represented by the green line) over the span of 4 minutes. I could determine arrival times and residence times for each interaction:

These analyses allowed me to discover several new biological facts. For example, a single SRP molecule can take on several distinct states when bound to a ribosome (characterized by their spectral properties as HIGH, INT -for intermediate-, and LOW). The image below summarizes hundreds of SRP changes on ribosomes, highlighting SRP’s three states and their different switching characteristics:

I could also show that how fast SRPs take to get to ribosomes (arrival-time), and how long they remain there (residence-time), depends on the nature of the ribosomes. The images below summarize thousands of interactions between SRPs and different ribosomes:

This work led to the publication of a research article (download here) in the Journal of Biological Chemistry on July 2014.
